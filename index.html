Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Although there is a lot of variability, there does appear to be a correlation between the poverty rate and the lack of healthcare. In states where the percentage of the population living in poverty is lower, the percantage of those without health care tends to be lower. In contrast, the states where those living in poverty is higher also tend to have a higher percentage of population without healthcare.

Texas is a clear outlier. It has the highest percentage without healthcare by far. Although, it does have a higher level of poverty than many other states, there are a number of other states that have a higher level of those in poverty, but have a lower percentage without healthcare.
